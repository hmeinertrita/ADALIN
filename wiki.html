Bellanore

8th January 2019 at 12:42pm
Cities Eida Qen Locations Quinn's Shelf

The city of Bellanore is located in the Quinn's Shelf region on the northern coast of Eida Qen. Despite it's arctic location, the climate within the city is always warm, thanks to the system of geothermal tunnels that run beneath the city.

History

Bellanore was built during the Civil Age by dwarven silver miners who discovered the naturally occuring Geothermal events. They built and designed the Bellanore Aeroducts.

The city's rich mining economy attracted wealthy settlers, including many Artificers, who wished to make use of the abundant supply of precious metals. This intial magical community quickly grew, attracting many scholars, mages, and religious organizations, including High Relic Assay, and Bellanore quickly became the centre of the academic community on Eida Qen.

The various scholars who resided in Bellanore would eventually create the Pact of the Leyline with the intention of furthering academic research purely for the betterment of society rather than personal gain.

The large community of arcanists eventually built a large workshop in the tunnels beneath the city. The workshop became known as the Ivuu-Galipsi Hylotheum.

At some point the Book of All passed through the city, before being teleported away, along with a young boy.

Present

After the destruction caused by Levana Longvine on the Day of Broken Road, which resulted in the disbanding of the Luna Republic, a new ruling body was instated. A council of religious leaders, known as the New Leyline Convocation, was created from the leaders who were seen as saviours of the people during the rebuilding efforts. The Convocation is composed of New Relic Ribbon, Mahant Novakovic, Weaver Marcario Pacheva, and Handmaiden Amias Braith, and is overseen by the Moderator.

Church of Bahamut

8th January 2019 at 12:42pm

Teachings of Bahamut: The way I’m going with Bahamut and Tiamat worship is it is really just all about ethics and morality. Its trying to answer questions like “what does it mean to be good?” where other gods are more definitive (i.e. “seek knowledge” or “protect nature”). So part of it is that the line between what’s Bahamut and what’s Tiamat gets real blurry a lot of the time, and it’s the Church’s job to figure out where that line is exactly. The result of this is there can be lots of debate about where to draw the line but given the current state of the Church, most followers haven’t had the luxury to engage in theological debates.

The way the Church of Bahamut is run and the way Bahamut worship works in general is almost like a strange combination of ethics philosophy and catholicism. There are no definitive “holy texts” but there are books and essays written by ancient priests which try to interpret what it means to be “just” and “merciful”, or what justice is in the eyes of Bahamut. The more popular texts are often revered and referenced to in the same way the Bible or other holy texts are referred to in real religions.

Most of the texts are based around interpreting The Two Tenets of Justice.

Notable Figures:

Odaheim Other Titles: Dragon of the North, Left Hand of the Platinum Dragon, Spirit of Mercy, Patron of the Repentant, Giver of Second Chances, Son of Justice Description: Odaheim was the first and only messenger of Bahamut, sent down to earth in ancient times to deliver the Two Tenets. He stayed on the mortal plane in order to guide the people of the world to the path of justice and mercy before disappearing centuries ago. No one has seen or heard from him since (except you guys kinda).

Recent History:

During the Troubled Age, Tiamat had agency all across the continent so the Church of Bahamut focused its efforts heavily on keeping her forces at bay during this time. As such, it didn’t involve itself with the violent political turmoils of the time and its influence waned as all of its resources were focused on the fight against Tiamat. The Church’s efforts were not in vain, in fact it prevented many catastrophes during this time. However, many of these tales have been forgotten outside the Church, due partly to its position outside of the limelight and partly to the passage of time. The Church has always prided itself on shielding the innocent from the corrupting influence of Tiamat, which is often a thankless task. Its paladins and clerics are taught to never pursue glory or power, but to simply be content with their station and the fact that their work is crucial to the security of the people.

Recently, as the continent has become more stable, some of the younger members of the Church have questioned the lack of involvement with politics and community. Some believe that the Church has a responsibility to the people to protect them from cruel tyrants and other threats. They believe the Church could accomplish more as an organization that participates in society rather than being simply a bystander in current events.

Circular travel: If you travel in one direction anywhere in the Feywild you eventually end up back where you start.

  • Travelling wardways from the daywind will have you pass through the Daysummer, Spring, and Wintersun regions before returning to where you started.
  • Travelling wardways from the nightwind will have you pass through the Dusksummer, Autumn, and Wintermoon regions before returning to where you started.
  • Travelling boundways from the summerwind will have you pass through the Daysummer and Dusksummer before returning to where you started.
  • Travelling boundways from the winterwind will have you pass through the Wintersun and Wintermoon before returning to where you started.
  • Travelling boundways from Spring or Autumn regions will have you remain in the Spring or Autumn regions before returning to where you started.

Cause & Effect Cause and effect is a big theme in terms of the gods of this world in such a way that it doesn’t really exist. Gods gain strength from their followers and their followers gain strength from their gods. Changes on the mortal plane mirror changes in the heavens and vice versa. Therefore, it is easier to think of gods and mortals as parallels of each other rather than as the cause or effect of each other.

The Nature of a God The gods in this setting are more like concepts that are given power and form by mortals. As such, the world of the gods and the world of mortals are very closely linked. The new gods are only as powerful as their following and their following is only as powerful as the god. This circular relationship is the cause of many philosophical debates (the equivalent of the “is god real?” debate). The nature and alignment of a god is also very much dependant on the worshipper. The gods are very multifaceted in this sense, a god which is traditionally good to its followers may appear as an malevolent being to a particular zealot.

Gods & Mortals The gods have a very strong connection to the mortal plane and to the mortals who worship them. It is not uncommon for the gods to contact their worshippers through dreams, visions, or even manifestation. However, a god will often appear different to each person, adopting a unique persona for their followers. This persona is usually indistinguishable from how they are traditionally depicted, but it is known to change drastically. A god will often form a very personal connection with the select few who have their favour and will contact them often.

The Heavens & The Mortal Plane The heavens and the mortal plane are said by many to exist in parallel. If a monumental event were to occur on one plane, an event of similar magnitude will happen on the other. The most obvious example of this is the changing of the Ages. When one Age ends and another begins, this correlates to a war in the heavens. The pantheon of the New Age will attempt to overthrow the pantheon of the Old Age. These events happen in parallel so neither of them are the cause or effect of the other.

The New Gods and the Races The creation of the Mortals at the start of the Second Age also caused the birth of the New Gods. Each of the races are associated with a particular god. The majority of a New God’s following is typically made up of the races associated with that god.
